WEEK 6

Sometime in the first week of March

I feel like this week went by like a blur, I “finished” the capacitive portion of the assignment earlier last week with Abby and Geoff, by finishing up a sensor set-up that senses change in volume. So, I had originally put that portion aside for a while and continued to my other sensor. (But I shouldn’t have done that… long story short the capacitive sensor didn’t really catch any meaningful readings.) The video below is from earlier in the week.

Putting that topic aside for a moment, the other sensor that I wanted to use was a hall sensor (at first). Kassia had mentioned that hall sensors are relatively easy to utilize. I didn’t realize at that moment that yes, it’s fairly easy to set-up, but I soon realized that the set-up alone was fairly easy to set, but the readings itself are actually quite finicky and weirdly sensitive? I think a challenge of this week is really understanding the calibration or the factors that make a sensor more or less sensitive.

I then decided to change that sensor to a microphone instead. For this, Isa helped me during her office hours to set up a microphone sensor. I was finally able to get soldering checked off by her as I practiced soldering four rungs to each other. I think I did it pretty well! We can ask Isa. Then, connecting the wires to the Arduino was really a matter of looking at tutorials like the one provided on the website.

Fast forwarding now to March 9th, the day the assignment is due, I tried to work the capacitive sensor again by going to the classroom on my own.
